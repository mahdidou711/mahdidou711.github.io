About Me

I am a Master's student in Embedded Systems and Computer Architecture (M1 E3A) at Université Paris-Saclay, one of France's top engineering universities. My work lives at the intersection of hardware and software: I design digital logic in VHDL, program microcontrollers without abstraction layers, and optimize code at the register level.

I started building things with microcontrollers during my undergraduate years in Algiers, where I assembled PCBs by hand and wrote firmware from scratch. That hands-on foundation drives everything I do today. Whether it is a custom STM32 driver, an FPGA arithmetic unit, or a lidar-driven autonomous car, I care about understanding how every layer of the system actually works.

Outside the lab, I train at the gym, explore FPGA projects for fun, and keep refining my Linux environment. I believe that engineers who stay curious outside of class build the best instincts inside it.
